Vitamin B12 is difficult to get reliably from plant-based foods unless products are fortified. Common fortified foods include some soy milks, breakfast cereals, nutritional yeast, and meat alternatives, but levels vary widely by brand.

Supplement labels commonly list cyanocobalamin, methylcobalamin, or hydroxocobalamin. The right approach depends on diet pattern, blood levels, symptoms, medicines, and medical history. Use product labels to confirm form, dose, capsule material, and testing information.

If a product uses capsules, check whether the capsule is gelatin or HPMC. Gelatin is animal-derived, while HPMC is commonly plant-based. When the source is not listed, GreenFit marks confidence lower rather than making a binary claim.
