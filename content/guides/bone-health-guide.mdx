Bone health is not only about calcium. A practical plan should also consider vitamin D status, vitamin K2 intake, protein adequacy, resistance training, sunlight exposure, and overall energy intake.

Calcium can come from calcium-set tofu, fortified soy milk, ragi, sesame, certain greens, and fortified foods. Check the nutrition panel because plant milks vary widely.

Vitamin D3 helps calcium absorption, but deficiency should be confirmed and managed with a qualified clinician. Vegan D3 is commonly lichen-derived. Vitamin K2 may support calcium handling, but product quality, dose, medications, and medical history matter.

For everyday meals, pair calcium-rich foods with enough protein and avoid very low-calorie dieting for long periods. Bone health is slow-moving, so consistency matters more than one perfect food.
