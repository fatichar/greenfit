Iron and zinc are important, but supplementation should not be guessed. Too much can cause problems, and symptoms can overlap with many conditions. Blood tests and medical guidance matter when deficiency is suspected.

Food sources include dals, chana, rajma, soy foods, nuts, seeds, whole grains, and fortified foods. Vitamin C-rich foods can help with non-heme iron absorption when eaten in the same meal.

Tea and coffee around meals can reduce iron absorption for some people. A practical approach is to separate them from iron-focused meals when deficiency is a concern.
