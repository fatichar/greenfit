Protein intake often falls short when meals are built mostly around rice, roti, poha, upma, or low-dal portions. The easiest fix is not a complicated supplement stack. It is making sure each meal has a clear protein anchor.

Good everyday anchors include dal, rajma, kala chana, kabuli chana, soy chunks, tofu, tempeh, besan, sprouts, peanuts, and fortified soy milk. Protein values vary by preparation, so treat numbers as approximate and use consistent portions.

For affordability, start with chana, rajma, masoor dal, moong dal, besan, peanuts, and soy chunks. For convenience, tofu, tempeh, soy curd, and plant protein powder can help when whole-food meals are hard to fit.

If replacing dairy, use tofu or tempeh instead of paneer, soy curd or peanut curd instead of curd, and fortified soy milk instead of milk. Oat milk can work for taste and cooking, but it is usually lower in protein unless fortified or blended.
